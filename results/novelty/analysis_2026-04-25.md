### 2.1 Sudoku — overfit detected

`novelty-trm-mlp-sudoku-seed0/trm_official_sudoku_train_log.csv`:

- Epoch 10: `val_puzzle_acc = 0.8484`, `val_cell_acc = 0.9161` — best score, never beaten.
- Epoch 50: val collapses to `0.6367` and stays there for 100+ epochs while train accuracy keeps climbing (0.961 → 0.979).
- Final epoch 150: val recovers slightly to `0.6720`. Still 18 points below the epoch-10 peak.

This is classic overfit, not noise. The HF init landed the model at its ceiling for this recipe; the remaining 140 epochs of training spent ~7.6 hours pulling the model away from a working solution. Practical implication: future iso-time sudoku runs should cap at ~30 epochs, freeing ~7 hours per machine.

### 2.2 Maze — under-budget by orders of magnitude

`novelty-trm-att-maze-seed0/stdout.log`:

```
Epoch 10/5000 - lm_loss: 0.5928  cell_acc: 0.9507  puzzle_acc: 0.0850  best: 0.2050  elapsed: 4.6h  ETA: 94.2d
[wall-clock] budget exhausted before epoch 17/5000
```

Per-epoch cost on RTX 5070 is ~27 minutes for maze attention. The 7-hour rig-1 cap allows ~16 epochs out of the 5000-epoch schedule. The README's "Known limitations" section flags this in principle; in practice the iso-time maze row is essentially the HF-init starting score.

### 2.3 Wall-clock budget overshoot

Both runs exceeded the rig-1 cap (25,200 s = 7 h):

- sudoku: 31,415 s (+25%)
- maze: 26,388 s (+5%)

Cause: trainer only checks the budget between epochs. Sudoku log shows a mid-run jump — epoch 50 → 60 elapsed went 2.3 h → 4.2 h, suggesting the machine was paused or contended. The kWh figures will read slightly high vs other rigs as a result; a footnote in the report should flag this.

## 3. K-vote inference results so far

Source: `results/novelty/k_vote_runs/trm-mlp-sudoku/emissions.csv` (TRM-MLP sudoku only — maze K-vote and all LLM K-vote not started).

| K | duration (s) | kWh | kWh / K | per-sample cost vs K=1 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 (run a) | 8,513 | 0.427 | 0.427 | 1.00× |
| 1 (run b) | 8,443 | 0.425 | 0.425 | 1.00× |
| 2 | 28,230 | 1.418 | 0.709 | 1.66× |
| 4 | 57,877 | 2.200 | 0.550 | 1.29× |

If the K samples were processed in a single vectorised batch the per-K column would be roughly flat near 0.42. Instead K=2 doubled the per-sample cost. The K=4 partial recovery (0.55) suggests *some* batching kicks in but not full vectorisation. Likely culprits to investigate in `scripts/run_novelty_k_vote.py`:

- Re-running the dataset/eval loop K times in series.
- Padding the K dimension into the batch but exceeding a memory threshold that forces a split.
- Re-loading the checkpoint per K.

**Projected cost** if the K=2/K=4 trend holds: K=8 ≈ 4.4 kWh, K=16 ≈ 8.8 kWh. Design budget assumed ~1.7 / ~3.4. Worth fixing before launching the K=8/K=16 sweep.
